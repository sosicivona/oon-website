# Product Brief

## Vision

The Oyagaa-Oomo Network website should become a modern, source-first public entry point for the OOMO/UMMO dossier.

The site should replace the front-door role of outdated Ummo websites without pretending to replace the archives themselves. It should orient visitors, build credibility through careful source handling, and route people toward documents, videos, archives, AI research prompts, and deeper research pathways.

## Audience

The primary audience is skeptical but curious visitors who have little or no context for OON or the OOMO/UMMO dossier.

Secondary audiences include:

- Researchers
- Viewers coming from OON videos
- People interested in document analysis
- Collaborators who may help with content, design, sources, or technical work
- People familiar with older Ummo archives who need a clearer modern entry point

## Problem

The OOMO/UMMO dossier is difficult to approach. Existing materials can feel scattered, dated, visually inconsistent, or hard to evaluate. New visitors may not know what they are looking at, what is primary source material, what is interpretation, or where to begin.

The website needs to help visitors understand the project quickly without overclaiming.

## UX Strategy

The site should use progressive disclosure.

8 seconds:

- Visitors understand that OON is a modern guide to the OOMO/UMMO dossier.
- Visitors understand that the site is source-first and critical, not belief-first.

60 seconds:

- Visitors understand what the dossier is.
- Visitors understand why it may deserve examination.
- Visitors understand how to approach it critically.
- Visitors know where to go next.

5 minutes:

- Visitors feel the quality and strangeness of the material through selected quotes, videos, themes, and document pathways.
- Visitors can distinguish source, translation, analysis, hypothesis, and OON interpretation.

Deep mode:

- Visitors can move into archives, primary documents, structured research prompts, document maps, and the broader source ecosystem.

## Site Goals

The website should help visitors:

- Understand what OON is
- Understand what the OOMO/UMMO dossier is
- Understand why it may deserve examination
- Approach the material critically
- Find curated source documents
- Watch OON videos
- Follow archive links
- Use AI research prompts responsibly
- Discover collaboration paths
- Enter deeper research pathways

## Landing Page Role

The homepage should work as a calm, credible orientation page. It should not try to prove the dossier, overwhelm visitors, or mimic old UFO aesthetics.

The homepage should answer:

- What is OON?
- What is the OOMO/UMMO dossier?
- Why look at it?
- How should I approach it?
- Where should I go first?

## Deep Archive Role

The site should not replace archives. Instead, it should route visitors toward them with context.

Archive and document areas should help visitors understand:

- What type of source they are seeing
- Whether a translation exists
- Whether references are verified
- How the material relates to themes or videos
- What remains uncertain

## Success Criteria

The project is successful if:

- A first-time visitor understands the purpose of the site within 8 seconds.
- A skeptical visitor feels respected, not recruited.
- Source material is clearly separated from interpretation.
- Visitors can find a next step within 60 seconds.
- The site routes people toward archives and primary materials responsibly.
- Ivona can review each stage without needing to manage technical details manually.
- Future collaborators can understand how to contribute safely.
