# OON Narrative Arc

This document defines the deeper narrative posture for the Oyagaa-Oomo Network website.

## Primary Public Posture

The website should remain calm, readable, and source-first.

The homepage thesis should not be disclosure, prophecy, proof, fear, or recruitment. It should be reading:

- read the corpus
- compare source chains
- keep questions open
- separate source text from analysis
- move from documents to themes, evidence questions, archives, and public discussion

## Narrative Layers

### Layer 1: Newcomer clarity

The first job is orientation.

The visitor should understand:

- this is a correspondence and documentary dossier
- OON is a reading/interface network
- archive sites remain the archives
- the site does not ask for belief
- there is a safe first reading path

### Layer 2: Reading-first structure

The corpus should be made readable before it is argued.

The reader should be able to enter through:

- historical letters
- later communications
- themes
- source-status notes
- evidence questions
- archive references

### Layer 3: Source continuity

The site should teach readers to ask:

- Where is this source located?
- What language layer am I reading?
- Is this translation, summary, analysis, or source text?
- What does OON interpret?
- What remains open?

### Layer 4: Sober disclosure posture

OON may carry a deeper sober / non-fairy-tale disclosure posture, but this should be a deeper narrative layer, not the homepage thesis.

This means:

- avoid fairy-tale framing
- avoid spectacle
- avoid fear-based disclosure language
- avoid "believe us" posture
- avoid turning the dossier into entertainment
- keep human responsibility and source review central

The deeper narrative can acknowledge that the dossier raises difficult questions about humanity, contact, civilization, ethics, and interpretation. It should do so without making claims the source record has not earned.

### Layer 5: Public channels and living discussion

OON public channels can carry discussion, updates, videos, and interpretive work.

The website should keep the slower reading interface separate from faster public discussion. Public channels may orient; the website should preserve source visibility.

## What The Story Is Not

The site is not:

- a proof campaign
- a debunking campaign
- a recruitment funnel
- a prophecy page
- a sensational UFO portal
- a replacement archive
- an official source account

## Future Content Arc

The mature site can move through this sequence:

1. What is the case?
2. How should I read it?
3. What is the corpus?
4. What are the essential documents?
5. What themes repeat?
6. What evidence questions can be examined?
7. Where do the archives live?
8. How does OON interpret and discuss the material?
